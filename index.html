Summary

Avoidable accidents are happening now! More than five million crashes occur on U.S. roads alone every year, and more than 30,000 of those are fatal. Foresight will offer sophisticated and affordable rear-facing camera and integrated display systems for motorcycles and scooters. This innovative technology will revolutionize the motorcycle industry by enhancing the rider experience and improving rider safety in ways never imagined. Our products combine a compact, wide-angle camera with a powerful micro-computer to stream high-definition video to either a dedicated video display or wirelessly to any Smartphone. Both the hard-wired and wireless models will improve rider safety by dramatically increasing the rider’s view of traffic or other hazards behind them and placing the display within their normal field of vision on the vehicle. The wireless version includes further enhancements such as voice navigation and collision avoidance systems. We are hopeful that, in addition to creating a superior riding experience, Foresight’s products will also significantly reduce the number of motorcycle accidents and injuries to riders..

The motorcycle industry generated approximately $32 billion in 2014 and modest but steady growth is predicted over the next several years. A thriving motorcycle industry, combined with our commitment to continuous technological innovation creates tremendous market potential for Foresight. Our initial focus will be on direct-to-consumer marketing of our ready-to-install camera and display systems. After proving the technology and strength of the consumer market, Foresight intends to pivot and license our products to the major motorcycle manufactures.

The company will be formed as a New Jersey Corporation based in Piscataway, NJ and will be owned by the founders and principal investors from whom we are seeking $500,000 for further research and development, establishment of scalable production capacity and setup of facilities.

Product

Soon, when a motorcycle is approaching a car from behind or is riding next to it, the rear vision camera of the car will be activated, displayed on screen and an audible warning will be triggered. In order to provide reliable information, like the speed at what the motorcycle is traveling and the distance from the car, the best way is by establishing a Vehicle to Vehicle communications architecture, so both vehicles will be capable of avoid accidents by taking automated decisions like maintaining the speed, waiting before making a turn, or speeding up to pass. On the other hand, motorcycles will get similar information from cars. Of course, for such technology to be part of the daily life, standards and regulations will be needed.

As our first step we have developed a rear vision and collision avoidance system composed by a high quality camera, No InfraRed (NoIR) filter for low light environments, a vehicle vicinity image processing algorithm for collision avoidance, compatible with different displays. The system is wired and waterproof and will be sold as a DIY installation kit. The rear vision camera solves the problem of blind spots when riding motorcycles. However, the system is not just a camera and display, it includes a small but powerful single board computer this computer will host the software of the system and includes capabilities for data storage by using a microSD card, this allowed us to feature the capture of images every time the collision avoidance warnings are triggered. Those captures can be retrieved later on by extracting the microSD card from the computer. In order to power the system, it will include a 12V adaptor. This product will be used as Minimum Viable Product in the proof of concept and market assessment, allowing Foresight to establish meaningful relationships with the consumers.

Our second step is the development of a stand-alone wireless camera and smartphone video streaming, a waterproof camera, capable of transmitting real time video, wirelessly to the Smartphone, uses an advanced video compression format that allows the optimization of wireless signal bandwidth, uses a powerful single board computer configured as a wireless hotspot (ad hoc network configured as Personal Area Network) dedicated to video and data transmission, it generates a WiFi signal without the need of data usage or Internet connection. Includes a mobile application that allows the user to switch between the rear vision camera image and other applications such as navigation, maps, and voice guidance. The camera includes the features of the rear vision and collision avoidance system, it also includes a set of proximity sensors for collision avoidance and an emergency system for collision detection that at the event of an emergency will alert the police or ambulance. The inclusion of a stand-alone network infrastructure in the motorcycle is a key element on taking the lead for the establishment of vehicle to vehicle communications.
